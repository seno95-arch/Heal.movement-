Frequently Asked Questions

What is the HEAL Movement about? ▼

The HEAL Movement is built on four core principles: Health (physical, mental, and emotional wellness), Empowerment (giving students a voice), Advocacy (standing up for student rights), and Leadership (transparent and accountable leadership). We are committed to building a stronger student community through these values.

When are the elections? ▼

The SLCMC SRC Elections are scheduled for 31 July 2026. Voting will take place from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM at designated voting centers on campus.

Who can vote? ▼

Any registered SLCMC student with a valid student ID is eligible to vote. If you're unsure about your registration status, please contact the election office before the voting date.

What are the candidate positions? ▼

The HEAL Movement candidates are running for the following positions: President (Calistus), Vice President (Elizabeth), Secretary (Lindsy), Treasurer (Markose), Sports & Recreation Officer (Mutabari), and Student Affairs Officer (Ochieng).

How can I support the HEAL Movement? ▼

You can support the HEAL Movement by voting on election day, sharing our manifesto with other students, attending campaign events, and spreading the word about our commitment to student welfare and leadership. Every voice counts!

What makes HEAL Movement different? ▼

The HEAL Movement stands out because we address student needs holistically—from mental health to academic excellence to sports development. We prioritize transparency, accountability, and servant leadership. Our candidates have already demonstrated their commitment through their actions on campus.
